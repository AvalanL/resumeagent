# Employment Gap Explainer Skill

Help users address resume gaps confidently and professionally.

## Trigger

User says `/gaps` or mentions employment gaps, time off, or career breaks.

## The Reality

**Good news:** Gaps are less stigmatized post-2020
**Bad news:** With 100+ applicants per role, gaps still get filtered

**Solution:** Acknowledge, explain briefly, pivot to value

## Types of Gaps

### 1. Caregiving
- Children, elderly parents, family illness
- **Frame as:** "Family caregiving" or "Primary caregiver"
- **Skills gained:** Time management, crisis management, patience

### 2. Health
- Personal illness, recovery, mental health
- **Frame as:** "Medical leave" (no details needed)
- **Note:** You're NOT required to disclose specifics

### 3. Layoff/Job Loss
- Company downsizing, restructuring
- **Frame as:** "Company restructuring" + what you did during gap
- **Skills gained:** Whatever you learned/did during the time

### 4. Education/Upskilling
- Degree, certification, bootcamp
- **Frame as:** Full-time education (list it!)
- **Easy win:** This isn't really a "gap"

### 5. Travel/Sabbatical
- Extended travel, personal development
- **Frame as:** "Career sabbatical" or "Professional development"
- **Skills gained:** Cultural awareness, adaptability, languages

### 6. Entrepreneurship
- Failed business, freelance, side projects
- **Frame as:** "Independent consultant" or "Founder"
- **Skills gained:** Everything you did, even if it didn't scale

### 7. Job Search
- Looking but not finding
- **Frame as:** What you did WHILE searching
- **Fill with:** Volunteering, courses, projects, freelance

### On the Resume

**Option 1: Address in Experience**
```

```

**Option 2: Functional Format**
Focus on skills, not timeline. Group by skill area rather than chronological.

**Option 3: Years Only**
Use years instead of months to minimize visible gaps.
```
Senior Manager | 2019 - 2022  (not Jan 2019 - Mar 2022)
```

### In Cover Letter

Address briefly, pivot to value:

```
"After taking time to [brief reason], I'm excited to return 
to [field] with fresh perspective and updated skills in [X]. 
During my time away, I [productive activity], which makes me 
even better prepared for this role."
```

### In Interviews

**The Formula:**
1. Acknowledge briefly (1 sentence)
2. What you gained/did (1-2 sentences)
3. Why you're ready now (1 sentence)
4. Pivot to your value (rest of answer)

**Example:**
"I took a year off to care for a family member. During that time, 
I kept my skills sharp by completing the Google PM certification 
and consulting on two freelance projects. I'm now fully focused 
on my next role and excited about this opportunity because..."

## Gap-Filler Ideas

If you have a gap, consider adding:

| Activity | How to List |
|----------|-------------|
| Online courses | "Professional Development" section |
| Freelance work | As a role: "Independent Consultant" |
| Volunteering | As experience with org name |
| Side projects | "Projects" section |
| Certifications | Education or Skills section |

## Output

For each gap, provide:
1. **Suggested framing** (how to describe)
2. **Resume language** (actual text)
3. **Cover letter line** (if needed)
4. **Interview answer** (what to say)
5. **Gap-filling activities** (if still in gap)
