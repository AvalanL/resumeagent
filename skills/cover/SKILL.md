# Cover Letter Skill

You write compelling, personalized cover letters that complement resumes.

## Trigger

User says `/cover` or needs a cover letter.

## Gather First

1. **Target job posting** (paste the listing)
2. **Company name**
3. **Why this company?** (interest, values, connection)
4. **Key qualification** they want to highlight

## Structure (One Page Max)

### Opening Paragraph (2-3 sentences)
- What role you're applying for
- One compelling reason you're a fit
- Hook that makes them keep reading

**Examples:**
- "When I saw [Company] was looking for a [Role], I knew my [specific experience] was exactly what you need."
- "After [specific accomplishment], I'm ready to bring that same impact to [Company]."

### Middle Paragraph(s) (2-3 short paragraphs)

**Paragraph 2: Your Best Match**
- Pick their top requirement
- Show specific evidence you meet it
- Include a metric if possible

**Paragraph 3: Why This Company**
- Something specific about them (not generic)
- How your values/goals align
- What you'll contribute

**Optional Paragraph 4: Address Concerns**
- Career change? Explain the pivot
- Gap? Brief positive framing
- Overqualified? Show genuine interest

### Closing Paragraph (2 sentences)
- Express enthusiasm
- Call to action (interview request)
- Thank them

## Format

```
[Your Contact Info — match resume]
[Date]

[Hiring Manager Name if known]
[Company Name]
[Address — optional]

Dear [Mr./Ms. Last Name / Hiring Manager],

[Opening paragraph]

[Middle paragraphs]

[Closing paragraph]

Sincerely,
[Your Name]
```

## Tone Guidelines

| Company Type | Tone |
|--------------|------|
| Startup | Energetic, casual, personality |
| Corporate | Professional, formal, precise |
| Creative | Show creativity, unique voice |
| Non-profit | Mission-driven, passionate |

## Power Phrases

**Opening hooks:**
- "My [X years] of [skill] have prepared me to..."
- "I've spent my career [doing X], and [Company]'s mission resonates deeply."
- "When I [specific achievement], I discovered my passion for [field]."

**Closing strong:**
- "I'd welcome the opportunity to discuss how my [skill] can contribute to [goal]."
- "I'm excited about the possibility of bringing [skill] to [Company]."

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Repeating the resume (add new context instead)
❌ Starting with "My name is..." (they know)
❌ Generic "I'm a hard worker" (be specific)
❌ Too long (one page max, 3-4 paragraphs)
❌ No company research (shows no interest)

## Output

Provide:
1. **Complete cover letter** (ready to copy)
2. **Personalization notes** (what to customize per application)
3. **Subject line** (if sending via email)

## Quick Customization Template

For rapid applications, fill in:

```
Dear [Hiring Manager],

I'm applying for [ROLE] at [COMPANY]. My experience in [RELEVANT SKILL] — including [SPECIFIC ACCOMPLISHMENT] — aligns perfectly with your need for [THEIR REQUIREMENT].

At [Previous Company], I [ACHIEVEMENT WITH METRIC]. I'm drawn to [COMPANY] because [SPECIFIC REASON — product, mission, culture].

I'd love to discuss how I can contribute to [THEIR GOAL]. Thank you for considering my application.

Sincerely,
[Name]
```
